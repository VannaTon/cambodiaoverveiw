WELCOME TO KRATIE PROVINCE



GENERAL

Kompong Cham located in eastern Cambodia along the serene banks of the Mekong River, is a province imbued with a rich tapestry of history, culture, and natural beauty. The provincial capital, also named Kompong Cham, exudes a unique charm with its blend of French colonial architecture and traditional Khmer influences. The remnants of colonial buildings alongside traditional wooden houses create a picturesque streetscape that tells the story of the province’s diverse heritage. This architectural blend is a visual testament to the province’s historical evolution and its ability to harmonize different cultural influences.


One of the most remarkable historical sites in Kompong Cham is Wat Nokor Bachey, an 11th-century temple complex. This temple uniquely combines ancient Angkorian architecture with more recent additions, offering a fascinating glimpse into Cambodia's religious and architectural history. The temple complex, with its intricate carvings and serene ambiance, stands as a testament to the spiritual significance and artistic achievements of the Khmer civilization. Visitors can wander through the ancient corridors, marveling at the detailed stonework that has withstood the test of time.


Kompong Cham is also known for its distinctive bamboo bridges, particularly the one connecting the mainland to Koh Paen island. These bridges are ingeniously rebuilt every dry season by local villagers using traditional techniques. The annual reconstruction of the bamboo bridge is not only a practical solution for connecting communities but also a cultural event that showcases the ingenuity and resourcefulness of the local people. Walking across these bridges offers a unique experience and a sense of connection to the traditional ways of life that continue to thrive in the province.
The province’s landscape is dominated by vast rubber plantations, lush fruit orchards, and expansive rice fields. These agricultural areas are the backbone of Kompong Cham’s economy, providing livelihoods for many local families. The sight of endless rows of rubber trees, the vibrant green of the rice paddies, and the abundance of tropical fruits reflect the region’s agrarian roots and its importance in Cambodia’s agricultural sector. Visitors can often see farmers at work, tending to their crops with techniques passed down through generations.


The Mekong River itself is a lifeline for Kompong Cham, offering not only scenic beauty but also essential resources for fishing, irrigation, and transportation. The riverbanks are bustling with activity, from fishermen casting their nets to boats ferrying goods and people across the water. The river provides a tranquil backdrop for the town and offers numerous opportunities for boat trips, allowing visitors to explore the surrounding landscapes and gain a different perspective on life in the province.


Kompong Cham town is home to vibrant markets that are a hub of local life. These markets are filled with the sights, sounds, and smells of daily commerce, offering everything from fresh produce and traditional Khmer foods to local crafts and textiles. Strolling through these markets provides a sensory feast and a chance to interact with local vendors and artisans, gaining insight into the daily lives and traditions of the people. The markets are not just places of trade but also social centers where the community gathers, making them an essential part of the local culture.


The people of Kompong Cham are renowned for their warmth and hospitality, often welcoming visitors with open arms and genuine friendliness. This welcoming spirit enhances the travel experience, allowing visitors to feel truly immersed in the local culture. Traditional festivals and ceremonies are held throughout the year, offering visitors the opportunity to witness and participate in the rich cultural traditions that define the region. These events often feature traditional music, dance, and religious rituals, providing a deep connection to the cultural heritage of Kompong Cham.

PLACE TO VISIT


Wat Nokor Bachey Temple Wat Nokor Bachey Temple, a testament to Cambodia's rich historical tapestry, was built in the 11th century under the reign of King Suryavarman II. This temple complex is located just a few kilometers from Kampong Cham town, making it easily accessible for visitors. The site is unique for its harmonious blend of ancient and modern religious architecture, symbolizing the continuity of faith and tradition in Cambodia. Historical Significance: The original temple, constructed from sandstone and laterite, features classic Angkorian architecture. The temple's intricate carvings depict scenes from Hindu mythology, particularly the Ramayana and Mahabharata epics. These carvings showcase the skilled craftsmanship of the Khmer artisans and offer insights into the religious and cultural life of the Angkorian period. Modern Additions: Within the temple grounds, a contemporary Buddhist pagoda has been constructed, where local monks reside and perform daily rituals. This juxtaposition of ancient and modern structures creates a unique ambiance, allowing visitors to experience the evolution of Cambodian religious practices. The modern pagoda is adorned with colorful murals and statues of the Buddha, providing a stark yet harmonious contrast to the ancient ruins. Visitor Experience: Exploring Wat Nokor Bachey, visitors can wander through the maze-like corridors of the old temple, discover hidden chambers, and marvel at the detailed bas-reliefs. The site is often less crowded than the temples of Angkor, offering a peaceful and contemplative atmosphere. The temple is also a place of active worship, and visitors may witness local ceremonies and festivals, adding a vibrant cultural dimension to their visit.

Koh Pen Island , nestled in the Mekong River, is a serene escape that embodies the rustic charm and natural beauty of rural Cambodia. This picturesque island is accessible via a seasonal bamboo bridge, an engineering marvel rebuilt annually after the rainy season, or by ferry when the bridge is submerged. Scenic Beauty: Koh Pen Island is a haven for nature lovers and those seeking a tranquil retreat. The island's landscape is dominated by lush rice paddies, swaying palm trees, and traditional stilt houses. The gentle rhythm of rural life here offers a stark contrast to the hustle and bustle of urban centers. Activities: Bicycle rentals are popular on the island, providing an ideal way to explore its scenic beauty at a leisurely pace. As you cycle around, you'll pass by local farms, small temples, and friendly villagers who often greet visitors with warm smiles. The island's sandy beaches along the riverbanks are perfect for picnicking, swimming, or simply relaxing and enjoying the serene environment. Cultural Immersion: Koh Pen Island offers a unique opportunity to experience traditional Cambodian life. Visitors can observe local farmers at work, participate in community activities, and even stay overnight in one of the homestays to gain deeper insights into rural Cambodian culture. The island is also known for its vibrant festivals, where locals celebrate with traditional music, dance, and food.

Phnom Pros and Phnom SreiPhnom Pros (Man Hill) and Phnom Srei (Woman Hill) are two culturally and spiritually significant hills located a short distance from Kampong Cham town. These hills are enveloped in local folklore and offer stunning vistas of the surrounding countryside. Legend and Cultural Significance: According to legend, Phnom Pros and Phnom Srei were created as a result of a competition between men and women to build hills in one night. The women, using a clever trick, won the competition, leading to the formation of Phnom Srei. This legend is often recounted by locals and adds a mythical charm to the site. Phnom Pros (Man Hill): Phnom Pros is more easily accessible, with a paved road leading to the summit. The hill is home to a large pagoda, several stupas, and a giant Buddha statue. The pagoda, known as Wat Phnom Pros, is an important religious site where locals come to pray and participate in ceremonies. The hill also features a serene garden and several smaller shrines, creating a peaceful environment for meditation and reflection. Phnom Srei (Woman Hill): Phnom Srei requires a bit more effort to reach, involving a climb up a series of stone steps. The effort is well worth it, as the summit offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. At the top, you'll find a tranquil temple where monks reside and perform daily rituals. The serene atmosphere and stunning vistas make Phnom Srei a popular spot for both locals and tourists seeking spiritual solace and natural beauty. Additional Attractions: For those with more time in Kampong Cham, the province offers a wealth of additional attractions: Kampong Cham Central Market: The bustling Central Market is a vibrant hub of local activity. Here, visitors can experience the sights, sounds, and smells of Cambodian daily life. The market offers a wide array of goods, from fresh produce and spices to handicrafts and clothing. It's also a great place to sample delicious street food, such as num pang (Cambodian sandwiches) and fresh tropical fruits. French Colonial Buildings: Kampong Cham town is dotted with well-preserved French colonial buildings, a reminder of Cambodia's colonial past. Walking through the town, visitors can admire the elegant architecture, with its distinctive blend of French and Khmer styles. Notable buildings include the Governor’s Residence and the Old French Lighthouse, both of which offer a glimpse into the town’s historical heritage. Bamboo Bridges: Besides the famous bamboo bridge to Koh Pen Island, Kampong Cham is home to other bamboo bridges that showcase traditional Cambodian engineering skills. These bridges are not only functional but also picturesque, offering great photo opportunities and a chance to experience a unique aspect of local life.
