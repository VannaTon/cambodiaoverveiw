WELCOME TO KRATIE PROVINCE



GENERAL

Kratie , a province in northeastern Cambodia, offers a serene and culturally rich escape that feels worlds away from the busier tourist hubs of the country. Anchored by the tranquil Mekong River, Kratie is a place where time seems to slow down, inviting visitors to immerse themselves in the rhythms of rural Cambodian life. The town of Kratie, with its population of just over 30,000, is a charming mix of faded colonial architecture and traditional Khmer wooden houses. The streets are lined with old shophouses, many of which date back to the French colonial period, their shutters and balconies bearing the marks of time. This architectural heritage gives the town a nostalgic ambiance, a reminder of a bygone era when the Mekong was a major artery of trade and communication.


The riverfront in Kratie is the heart of the town, where locals gather in the early mornings and late afternoons to walk, chat, and relax by the water. The Mekong here is broad and slow-moving, its waters shimmering under the warm Cambodian sun. This part of the river is famous for being one of the last refuges of the critically endangered Irrawaddy dolphin. The dolphins, known for their rounded heads and gentle demeanor, are a major draw for visitors. Just a short boat ride from Kratie town, near the village of Kampi, you can see these rare creatures in their natural habitat, gliding gracefully through the river. The experience is both humbling and awe-inspiring, as the dolphins surface near your boat, their presence a symbol of the Mekong's rich biodiversity and the delicate balance of life that exists here.


Beyond its natural wonders, Kratie is also a province steeped in history and culture. The area is dotted with ancient pagodas and temples, many of which are still active centers of worship and community life. Wat Rokar Kandal, one of the oldest pagodas in the province, is particularly notable for its intricate wooden carvings and serene atmosphere. The temple's location by the river adds to its spiritual significance, making it a peaceful spot for reflection and meditation. In the surrounding countryside, small villages offer a glimpse into traditional Khmer life, with rice paddies stretching as far as the eye can see, and farmers going about their daily routines in harmony with the natural world.


Kratie’s market is another highlight, offering a sensory feast of sights, sounds, and smells. Here, vendors sell everything from fresh fruits and vegetables to handmade crafts and textiles, providing a vibrant snapshot of local life. The market is also a great place to sample traditional Cambodian food, with stalls offering dishes like amok (a type of curry), bai sach chrouk (pork and rice), and fresh Mekong fish grilled to perfection. The flavors are simple yet rich, reflecting the region’s connection to the land and the river.


One of Kratie’s most unique features is Koh Trong, a small, peaceful island in the middle of the Mekong River, just a short ferry ride from Kratie town. Koh Trong offers a perfect escape for those seeking tranquility and a deeper connection with nature. The island is home to a few small villages, where life moves at a slow, unhurried pace. Visitors can explore the island by bicycle or ox-cart, passing through orchards of pomelos, mangoes, and bananas, and witnessing daily life that has remained unchanged for generations. The island is also known for its community-based tourism initiatives, which allow visitors to stay in homestays, giving them a unique opportunity to experience local life firsthand.


Kratie’s appeal lies in its authenticity and its ability to transport visitors to a simpler time, where the connection to nature and community is still strong. The province's blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm, welcoming people make it a destination that leaves a lasting impression. Whether you're watching the sun set over the Mekong, listening to the sounds of the forest on Koh Trong, or marveling at the grace of the Irrawaddy dolphins, Kratie offers a peaceful and enriching experience that captures the essence of Cambodia.

PLACE TO VISIT


Koh Trong is a serene, riverine island located in the middle of the Mekong River, just opposite the town of Kratie. This small, verdant island is a perfect escape for those seeking tranquility and an authentic experience of rural Cambodian life. The island is only about 6 kilometers (3.7 miles) long and 2 kilometers (1.2 miles) wide, making it easy to explore in a day. The best way to experience Koh Trong is by renting a bicycle or hiring an ox-cart, which allows you to leisurely traverse the island’s narrow paths and unpaved roads. As you cycle or ride around the island, you’ll pass through lush orchards where pomelos, mangoes, and bananas grow in abundance. The island is renowned for its pomelos, a type of citrus fruit, and the locals take great pride in their cultivation. Along the way, you’ll encounter traditional Khmer wooden houses raised on stilts, surrounded by small gardens and rice fields. The villagers on Koh Trong live a simple, agricultural lifestyle, and their warm hospitality is one of the island’s highlights. Many of the families participate in community-based tourism initiatives, offering homestays that provide visitors with a unique opportunity to experience local life firsthand. Staying in a homestay on Koh Trong allows you to learn about traditional farming practices, participate in cooking local dishes, and engage with the island’s community in a meaningful way. One of the island’s notable attractions is the “Tree Planting Project,” an eco-tourism initiative that encourages visitors to plant a tree as a contribution to the island’s reforestation efforts. The project has led to the planting of thousands of trees, which not only helps the environment but also leaves a lasting legacy for future visitors. Additionally, the island has a small pagoda at its northern tip, where you can enjoy peaceful views of the river and the mainland beyond. Koh Trong’s charm lies in its peaceful atmosphere, where the only sounds are the rustling of leaves in the wind, the songs of birds, and the occasional chatter of villagers. It’s a place where you can disconnect from the hustle and bustle of modern life and immerse yourself in the natural beauty and slow pace of rural Cambodia.

The Kampi Dolphin Pool is one of the most famous and significant attractions in Kratie, known for being one of the last remaining habitats of the critically endangered Irrawaddy dolphins. These freshwater dolphins, characterized by their rounded heads and gentle demeanor, are a rare sight and have become a symbol of the Mekong River’s ecological richness. Located about 15 kilometers (9 miles) north of Kratie town, the Kampi Dolphin Pool is one of the best places along the Mekong River to observe these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat. The journey to Kampi takes you through picturesque countryside, with rice fields, palm trees, and traditional villages lining the route. Upon arrival, you’ll board a small wooden boat that will take you out onto the river, guided by an experienced local boatman. The boat trip is a peaceful experience in itself, as you glide along the calm waters of the Mekong, surrounded by the sounds of nature. The best times to visit are early in the morning or late in the afternoon when the dolphins are most active. Spotting the Irrawaddy dolphins is a magical experience. These shy and elusive creatures usually appear in small pods, surfacing to breathe and occasionally playing near the boat. The sight of their smooth, grey bodies breaking the water’s surface is both thrilling and serene. The dolphins are considered sacred by local communities, who believe that they bring good luck and protection. As you watch them, it’s easy to understand why they are revered—their presence is a reminder of the Mekong’s natural beauty and the importance of conservation efforts to protect this fragile ecosystem. The Kampi Dolphin Pool is not just about the dolphins; it also offers stunning views of the Mekong River, with its wide expanse of water, dotted with small islands and sandbars. The riverbanks are lined with dense vegetation, creating a lush backdrop that enhances the sense of being in a pristine, untouched environment. After the boat trip, visitors can relax at one of the nearby floating platforms, where you can enjoy a refreshing drink and take in the serene river views.

Wat Rokar Kandal is one of the oldest and most historically significant pagodas in Kratie, offering a fascinating glimpse into the region’s spiritual and cultural heritage. This ancient Buddhist temple, located just a short distance from Kratie town, is believed to date back to the early 19th century. The pagoda is renowned for its unique architectural style, which combines traditional Khmer design with influences from Laos and Vietnam, reflecting the diverse cultural history of the region. The central vihara (temple hall) of Wat Rokar Kandal is a stunning example of traditional Khmer craftsmanship. The wooden structure is intricately carved with detailed patterns and motifs, depicting scenes from Buddhist mythology, as well as floral designs and geometric patterns. The roof is supported by large, intricately carved wooden pillars, and the walls are adorned with faded murals that tell the stories of the Buddha’s life and teachings. These murals, though weathered by time, still retain their vibrant colors and intricate details, offering a window into the artistic and religious traditions of the past. One of the most intriguing aspects of Wat Rokar Kandal is its historical significance. The pagoda has been a center of Buddhist worship and community life for over two centuries, surviving wars, political upheavals, and natural disasters. It is considered a sacred site by the local community, who continue to use the temple for religious ceremonies, festivals, and daily prayers. The temple’s serene atmosphere is palpable, and visitors often find themselves drawn to the peaceful energy that pervades the site. Wat Rokar Kandal also houses a small museum that showcases a collection of religious artifacts, including ancient Buddha statues, ceremonial objects, and traditional Khmer textiles. The museum offers insights into the spiritual practices and cultural heritage of the region, making it a valuable resource for those interested in learning more about Cambodia’s rich history. The temple is set amidst a tranquil garden, with tall trees providing shade and a gentle breeze rustling through the leaves. The grounds are meticulously maintained, with stone pathways leading visitors around the complex. There is a sense of timelessness at Wat Rokar Kandal, where the past and present coexist in harmony. As you explore the temple and its surroundings, you can feel the deep spiritual connection that has been nurtured here for generations. Visitors to Wat Rokar Kandal often leave with a sense of peace and reverence, having experienced a place where history, culture, and spirituality come together in a unique and beautiful way. The pagoda is a testament to the resilience of Cambodia’s cultural heritage and the enduring power of faith in the lives of its people.
