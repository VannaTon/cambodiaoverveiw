WELCOME TO KOMPONGTHOM PROVINCE



GENERAL

Kompong Spue, also known as Kampong Speu, is a province in central Cambodia, located to the west of the capital city, Phnom Penh. Renowned for its stunning landscapes, Kampong Speu boasts a mix of rolling hills, expansive rice paddies, and the dense, green expanses of the Cardamom Mountains. The province's natural beauty is complemented by its rich cultural heritage, making it a fascinating destination for visitors. One of Kampong Speu’s most notable products is its high-quality palm sugar, often regarded as the best in Cambodia. This sugar, harvested from the province’s numerous sugar palm trees, is a key ingredient in many traditional Cambodian dishes and desserts.


The process of making palm sugar, which involves collecting sap from the trees and boiling it down into a concentrated syrup, is a skill passed down through generations, adding to the province’s cultural richness. The province also offers a wealth of historical and cultural attractions. Ancient temples, such as the Phnom Preah and Phnom Sruoch, dot the landscape, providing glimpses into Cambodia’s historical and religious past. These sites attract tourists and history enthusiasts alike, offering a tranquil retreat from the bustling urban centers. Kampong Speu’s strategic location near Phnom Penh makes it a popular destination for city dwellers seeking a respite in nature.


The proximity allows for easy day trips or weekend getaways, where visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, bird watching, and exploring the province's numerous waterfalls and natural reserves. In recent years, Kampong Speu has seen significant development, with improvements in infrastructure, roads, and public services enhancing the quality of life for its residents and visitors. There is also an increasing focus on sustainable tourism and agriculture, aiming to preserve the natural environment while promoting economic growth. Efforts are being made to balance development with conservation, ensuring that the province's natural beauty and cultural heritage are protected for future generations. Overall, Kampong Speu represents a harmonious blend of natural splendor, cultural depth, and developmental progress. Whether it's for its delectable palm sugar, its historical sites, or its scenic landscapes, Kampong Speu continues to be a province of growing interest and significance in Cambodia.


Kampong Speu's cultural vibrancy is reflected in its festivals and traditional events, which are celebrated with great enthusiasm. These events provide a window into the local customs and traditions, fostering a sense of community and continuity. The province's culinary offerings, influenced by its agricultural produce, add another layer to its cultural richness.

PLACE TO VISIT


In Anlong Veng district, along Road 68, about 100 kilometers east of Oddar Meanchey provincial town, is a house once owned by former Khmer Rouge Commander Ta Mok. From 1979 until late 1997, this area was organized and controlled by the Khmer Rouge armies. Some remaining statues describe the way the rebels lived and how they arranged their troops during their bloody struggle against the government. In addition, in the forest is a pile of tires which is believed to be the funeral pyre and burial site of Pol Pot, the leader of Khmer Rouge. Many tourists go to Anlong Veng to see and photograph the sites and activities associated with the former regime, and the government has discussed turning this area into a historical site to attract even more tourists.

Champei waterfall or Laang Ta Thong waterfall is 35 kilometers northeast of Odda Meanchey provincial town. A natural site, is includes a huge boulder that measures 500 meters squar. There is also a mountain valley on both sides of the waterfall and the surrounding area is full of flowers, particulary orchids. About 500 meters from the waterfall, there is a natural and cultural site organized by the monks who meditate at the site. Known as Ruot Champei, it has a number of statues that describe the life process from birth until death, as well as other religious and wildlife statues. The provincial tourism office is organizing and developing other attractions in Oddar Meanchey province, including: Tonle Sar site, O'Angkrong site, Sneung temple, Tropiang Baray, Por Temple, Kuy Plantation, Beung Snor, Tumnub Thmei and Toul Kruoy, a killing field during the Khmer Rouge regime and the site of a Khmer Rouge prison.

O'Smach is on the Cambodian-Thai border, about 41 kilometers north of Oddar Meanchey provincial town. The town features a resort which includes a number of casinos, good restaurants and luxury hotels. The resort has been organized by the Royal Group and VIP Shop Group companies.The O Smach Resort has an impressive entry complete with a giant glowing green lotus set in a fountain. The resort has beautifully maintained grounds which feature, for some unknown reason, two military armoured vehicles decorated with Christmas lights. The O Smach resort has a casino and 198 hotel rooms and suites. Room facilities include satellite tv, aircon, en suite, mini bar etc.The resort facilities include a restaurant, 24 hour coffee shop, swimming pool, gym, sauna and massage parlour. Across the road is the Royal Hill Resort, offering yet another casino and hotel complex. Prices and facilities are similar to those at the O Smach Resort.The gaming rooms and interior of the Royal Hill Resort are rather utilitarian, in stark contrast with the impressive entry arch way and shrine in the grounds.O Smach in Cambodia is located 100 metres over the border checkpoint at Kap Choeng, Thailand.
